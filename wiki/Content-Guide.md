# Content Guide

## Content Sections

### Last 10 Nights and Laylatul Qadr

Special duas and focus for the most blessed nights of Ramadan. In these nights is Laylatul Qadr, the night better than a thousand months. The section includes:

- Duas to repeat in the search for Laylatul Qadr
- Reflection on the importance of these nights
- Qur'anic and hadith-based content with clear references

### Qur'anic Rabbana Duas

Powerful supplications that Allah placed in His Book. These duas gather deep meanings in very few words and cover:

- Guidance and mercy
- Forgiveness and repentance
- Steadfast hearts
- Righteous families
- Good in this life and the next

Each dua includes surah and ayah reference with links to Quran.com.

### Morning Adhkar

Authentic remembrance to start the day, including:

- Morning opening (Bismillah, protection)
- Morning protection
- Morning praise (tahlil, tasbih, etc.)

Sources include Sahih Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, Jami` at-Tirmidhi, Sunan Abi Dawud, and others.

### Evening Adhkar

Authentic remembrance to close the day, including:

- Evening opening
- Evening protection
- Before sleep (closing adhkar)

Same rigorous sourcing as morning adhkar.

## Languages

- **Arabic** — Full harakat (diacritics) for correct pronunciation
- **English** — Translation for understanding
- **Urdu** — Translation where available

## Sources

All content is linked to primary sources:

- **Quran.com** — For Qur'anic verses (surah and ayah)
- **Sunnah.com** — For hadith (Bukhari, Muslim, Tirmidhi, Abu Dawud, etc.)

The site includes a dedicated [Sources](/sources) page with the full reference list.

## Authenticity

- Qur'anic duas: direct from the Qur'an with surah/ayah
- Adhkar: only from authentic hadith (sahih, hasan)
- No unverified or weak content
